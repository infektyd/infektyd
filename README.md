# Hans (infektyd)

I work on local-first systems and AI infrastructure that stays on your own hardware. Most of my time goes into native Swift work, low-level systems, and building tools that solve specific technical problems in a practical way.

The projects here reflect different things I've explored:
- Binary Forge: NASM and low-level hardware control
- Syntra: Attempts at more structured reasoning and evaluation
- llmHub: Native macOS and iOS AI client work
- Foundation Writing and related experiments: Turning technical documents (like wiring diagrams) into interactive tools

I tend to favor explicit state, local-first design, and solutions that remain inspectable. I don't believe in building layers that can be replaced by the next foundation model. The goal is systems that are useful and stay under your control.

If you're working on similar problems (local AI, native execution, or turning complex documents into usable tools), feel free to open an issue or reach out. I'm happy to discuss technical approaches.

Current focus: Cleaning up and finishing some of the older experiments so they can be used more reliably.
